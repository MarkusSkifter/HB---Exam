\section*{Model}

Workers live for $T$ periods ($t \in \{1,\ldots,T\}$) and differ by birth cohort $b \in \{0,1,2,3,4\}$, with calendar year $y_t = t + b$. Each period they choose labor supply $l_t \in [0,1]$. Human capital $k_t \in \mathbb{R}_+$ accumulates endogenously and wages are subject to aggregate shocks $\lambda(y_t)$. The recursive problem for $1 \le t < T$ is

\[
V_t(b,k_t) = \max_{l_t \in [0,1]} \Bigl\{ U(c_t, l_t) + \beta\,\mathbb{E}_t\bigl[V_{t+1}(b, k_{t+1})\bigr] \Bigr\}
\]

subject to
\begin{align*}
k_{t+1} &= \bigl[(1-\delta)k_t + l_t\bigr]\varepsilon_{t+1}, \qquad \ln\varepsilon_t \sim \text{iid}\;\mathcal{N}\!\left(-\tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2,\,\sigma^2\right) \\
c_t      &= (1-\tau)\,w_t\,l_t + \pi_0 \cdot \mathbf{1}(l_t = 0) \\
w_t      &= \exp(\alpha_b + \gamma k_t)\cdot\lambda(y_t), \qquad \lambda(y_t) = 1 + \lambda_0 y_t
\end{align*}

with preferences
\[
U(c_t, l_t) = \frac{c_t^{1-\rho}}{1-\rho} - \phi\,\frac{l_t^{1+\eta}}{1+\eta}.
\]

At $t = T$ there is no bequest motive: $V_T(b,k_T) = \max_{l_T}\{U(c_T,l_T)\}$ subject to $c_T = (1-\tau)w_T l_T + \pi_0\cdot\mathbf{1}(l_T = 0)$.

\medskip
\noindent For simulation, initial human capital is drawn as $k_{i,0} = \min\{\max\{\tilde{k}_{i,0},0\},3\}$ with $\tilde{k}_{i,0}\sim\mathcal{N}(0.5,1)$, birth cohorts are drawn uniformly from $\{0,\ldots,4\}$, and $N=50{,}000$ individuals are simulated for $T=20$ periods.
